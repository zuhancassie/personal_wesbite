Research Focus

Specialized research agenda for funded graduate study.

My research direction sits at the intersection of electrical energy systems, machine learning, and hardware-aware engineering. The goal is to build models and systems that are accurate, interpretable, and useful for real-world power and industrial applications.

01

Digital Twins for Energy Systems

I am interested in digital twin models that connect physical energy assets with data-driven monitoring, forecasting, and real-time control. This includes hybrid modeling, simulation-informed learning, sensor feedback, and decision support for reliable energy operation.

  • Real-time modeling and control workflows
  • Forecasting under uncertainty and changing demand
  • Integration of software, hardware, and IoT data streams
02

Machine Learning for Power and Energy Forecasting

My conference work includes regional energy forecasting using transfer learning and metaheuristic optimization. I aim to extend this direction toward robust models that generalize across regions, seasons, and imperfect datasets.

  • Deep learning and transfer learning for load and energy data
  • Optimization-based hyperparameter search
  • Performance evaluation for practical forecasting deployment
03

Fault Diagnosis in Power Electronics

I work on data-driven fault classification in inverter systems, including single-phase and multilevel topologies. This direction supports safer, more reliable converter operation in renewable energy, industrial drives, and smart grid applications.

  • Simulation-based fault datasets using MATLAB/Simulink
  • Feature extraction and comparative ML evaluation
  • Topology-aware multiclass fault analysis
04

Human-Centered Intelligent Engineering Tools

Through my ERA voice assistant project, I explored ML, NLP, embedded design, and response routing to support academic and administrative communication. This reflects my broader interest in intelligent engineering tools that improve accessibility and workflow quality.

  • ML-NLP hybrid classification pipelines
  • Embedded deployment using Raspberry Pi
  • AI-assisted academic and departmental services
